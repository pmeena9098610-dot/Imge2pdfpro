Why Convert Photos to PDF?

Before diving into the how-to, let's understand why PDF is the preferred format for sharing images and documents:

  • Universal Compatibility: PDF files open on any device, any operating system — Windows, Mac, Linux, Android, iOS — without needing special software.
  • Fixed Layout: Unlike images that may display differently on different screens, PDFs maintain their exact layout, fonts, and formatting everywhere.
  • Multi-Page Support: You can combine dozens of photos into a single, organized document — perfect for portfolios, reports, or applications.
  • Smaller File Size: A well-compressed PDF can be significantly smaller than multiple individual image files, making it easier to email or upload.
  • Professional Appearance: Government offices, universities, and businesses all prefer PDF format for official submissions.

Method 2: Convert Photo to PDF on Android

Android devices offer several built-in and browser-based methods to convert photos to PDF. Here are the best approaches for Samsung, Xiaomi, OnePlus, Pixel, and other Android phones:

Option A: Using PhotoSePDF.in (Recommended)

  1. Open Chrome and go to photosepdf.in.
  2. Tap "Select Images" → choose photos from Gallery.
  3. Reorder if needed, then tap "Create PDF".
  4. Your PDF downloads instantly to the Downloads folder.

Option B: Using Google Drive Scan

  1. Open the Google Drive app.
  2. Tap the "+" button → select "Scan".
  3. Point your camera at the document or photo.
  4. Tap the checkmark to save as PDF to Google Drive.

Note: Google Drive's scan feature requires an internet connection and uploads your document to Google's cloud servers. If privacy is a concern, use the browser-based method instead.

Option C: Using the Print Function

  1. Open the photo in your Gallery app.
  2. Tap Share → select "Print".
  3. Choose "Save as PDF" as the printer option.
  4. Tap the save icon to export the PDF file.

Method 3: Convert Photo to PDF on iPhone & iPad

iOS users have powerful built-in tools for photo-to-PDF conversion, plus the option to use web-based converters for more flexibility:

Option A: Using PhotoSePDF.in on Safari

  1. Open Safari and navigate to photosepdf.in.
  2. Tap "Select Images" and choose photos from your Photo Library.
  3. Arrange photos, choose quality settings.
  4. Tap "Create PDF" → save to Files app.

Option B: iOS Print-to-PDF Trick

  1. Open the Photos app and select the image(s).
  2. Tap Share → scroll down and tap "Print".
  3. On the print preview screen, pinch outward (zoom in) on the preview thumbnail with two fingers.
  4. This opens a full-screen PDF preview. Tap Share again → "Save to Files".

Option C: Using the Files App

  1. Save your photos to the Files app first.
  2. Open Files, select multiple images by long-pressing and choosing "Select".
  3. Tap the three-dot menu (⋯) → "Create PDF".
  4. iOS will automatically generate a multi-page PDF from your selected images.

Method 4: Convert Photo to PDF on Windows PC

Windows 10 and 11 have a built-in virtual PDF printer that makes conversion incredibly simple:

Option A: Microsoft Print to PDF

  1. Right-click the photo file(s) in File Explorer.
  2. Select "Print" from the context menu.
  3. In the Printer dropdown, choose "Microsoft Print to PDF".
  4. Select your paper size, quality, and how many photos per page.
  5. Click "Print" → choose a save location → click "Save".

Option B: Using PhotoSePDF.in in Your Browser

Simply open PhotoSePDF.in/photo-to-pdf in any Windows browser. The advantage over the Print method is more control over layout, compression, and multi-image arrangements.

Method 5: Convert Photo to PDF on Mac

macOS includes the excellent Preview app which handles PDF creation natively:

  1. Open your photo in the Preview app (it's the default image viewer).
  2. Go to File → "Export as PDF".
  3. Choose your save location and filename → click "Save".

For multiple photos, select all images in Finder, right-click → "Quick Actions" → "Create PDF". macOS will merge them into a single PDF automatically. Alternatively, use PhotoSePDF.in in Safari for more customization options.

Best Settings for Different Use Cases

Different situations call for different PDF settings. Here's a quick guide:

  • Government Forms & Applications: Use A4 page size, high quality, and compress to under 500KB if file size limits apply.
  • College Assignments: A4 size, medium compression, portrait orientation. Use Merge PDF to combine multiple assignments into one file.
  • Photo Portfolio: Letter or A3 size, maximum quality, landscape orientation for wider photos.
  • ID Documents (Aadhar, PAN): A4 size with proper margins. Check our dedicated Aadhar Card PDF guide.
  • Email Attachments: Use higher compression to keep the file under 10MB — most email providers reject larger attachments.

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Running into problems? Here are solutions to the most common issues:

  • PDF file is too large: Use the Compress PDF tool to reduce file size. You can target specific sizes like 100KB for government portals.
  • Photos appear rotated: Before converting, make sure your photos are correctly oriented. Most tools auto-detect EXIF rotation data.
  • Blurry images in PDF: Select "High Quality" or "Original" quality settings. Avoid using heavily compressed source images.
  • Wrong page order: Use the drag-and-drop feature to reorder photos before creating the PDF.
  • Need to split a large PDF: Use our Split PDF tool to break a multi-page document into individual files.
