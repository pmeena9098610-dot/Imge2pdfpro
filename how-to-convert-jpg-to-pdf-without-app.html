Method 2: Windows Built-in Print to PDF

Windows 10 and 11 include a virtual PDF printer that can convert any printable file to PDF — no app needed:

  1. Right-click your JPG file in File Explorer → select "Print".
  2. In the Printer dropdown, choose "Microsoft Print to PDF".
  3. Select paper size and orientation. For multiple images, select all before right-clicking.
  4. Click "Print" → choose a filename and location → "Save".

Limitation: This method offers minimal control over image positioning, quality, and margins. For more customization, use PhotoSePDF.in.

Method 3: Mac Preview (No App Needed)

  1. Open your JPG image in Preview (double-click the file).
  2. Go to File → "Export as PDF".
  3. Choose save location and click "Save".

For multiple JPGs, select them all in Finder → right-click → "Quick Actions" → "Create PDF".

Method 4: iPhone Print Trick (No App)

iOS has a hidden PDF creation feature that most people don't know about:

  1. Open the JPG image in the Photos app.
  2. Tap the Share button (square with arrow).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Print".
  4. On the print preview, pinch outward (spread two fingers apart) on the thumbnail.
  5. This opens a PDF preview! Tap Share again → "Save to Files".

For a better experience with more options, simply use PhotoSePDF.in in Safari instead.

Method 5: Android Share Menu (No App)

  1. Open the JPG in your Gallery app.
  2. Tap Share → select "Print".
  3. Change the printer to "Save as PDF".
  4. Tap the download/save button.
