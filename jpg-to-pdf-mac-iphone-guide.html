By The Img2PDF SEO Team • 5 min read

The Apple Ecosystem Pain Point

Apple devices naturally capture glorious, high-definition photographs using advanced codecs like HEIC. While stunning on your retina display, trying to consolidate them into a universally acceptable document (PDF) for universities or agencies can be incredibly frustrating.

Method 1: Native macOS Preview (Slow)

On your MacBook, you can technically use the Preview app. You'd need to select all images, open them in a single Preview window, click 'File', hold the Option key to reveal 'Save As', and select PDF. However, you have zero control over margins, orientation fixing, or compression rules, resulting in 100MB+ PDF files that are impossible to email.

Method 2: Img2PDF Safari Engine (Fast & Optimized)

Instead of battling with Mac's Preview or downloading shady iOS App Store products loaded with subscriptions, open PhotoSePDF.in right inside Safari.

  • Airdrop Elimination: No need to Airdrop to your Mac first. Convert directly on your iPhone.
  • Sandbox Privacy: Safari runs our WebAssembly Engine. Your photos never leave your device natively.
  • Auto-Compression: We compress the massive Apple files to manageable, standard email sizes.
