How to Resize Photo & Signature for SSC, UPSC (100KB Limits)

Filling out government applications is stressful enough without having your photo and signature rejected because the "File Size is Too Large". Here is the ultimate 1-minute trick to compress them perfectly for any Indian Sarkaari Form.

The Golden Rules for Govt Forms

Most Indian recruitment boards (like SSC, UPSC, RRB, and State Police) have strict criteria:

  • Photographs: Must be recently taken, clearly visible without glasses/caps, and generally between 20KB to 50KB (sometimes up to 100KB for PDF uploads).
  • Signatures: Must be signed on white paper with a black pen, ranging heavily between 10KB to 20KB.
  • Certificates (Marksheets/Aadhar): Often required in PDF format, strictly under 100KB to 200KB.

Why Mobile Cameras Fail at This

Modern smartphones take pictures that are instantly 4MB to 6MB in size. Scanning your 10th marksheet with your phone camera creates a file that is 50 times larger than what the UPSC portal will accept. Using random compressor apps from the Play Store often ruins the text clarity, making your form liable for rejection.
