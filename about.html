About PhotoSePDF.in

India's leading client-side, 100% private PDF utility suite.

🇮🇳

Made in India, Processed Offline

We believe that your personal documents—like Aadhaar, PAN card, or passport photos—should never be uploaded to third-party internet servers. That is why PhotoSePDF operates entirely inside your browser's local memory.

Our Mission

PhotoSePDF.in was founded to bridge the gap between high-utility PDF converters and strict user privacy. Millions of Indian students, job seekers, and cyber cafe operators need to convert images to PDF, compress files to 100KB, or split/merge documents daily for government portals. Traditional online tools upload files to remote servers, risking data leaks. Our tools process everything 100% client-side in a single click, without requiring internet uploads or software installation.

Key Pillars of our Platform

  • 100% Client-Side Processing: No files leave your device. All calculations, image resizing, and PDF creations are done locally via standard web libraries.
  • No Registration Required: No login, no signup, and zero friction. Just drop your files and get your outputs immediately.
  • Optimized for Sarkaari Forms: Specific presets for Indian government portals (SSC, UPSC, NEET, Aadhaar, PAN card compression sizes under 100KB/200KB).
  • PWA & Mobile Ready: Add the app to your home screen and use it offline in remote villages without an active internet connection.

Technical Architecture & Security Model

To guarantee complete data privacy and high processing speeds, PhotoSePDF.in implements a decentralized local-first conversion model:

  • Local JS Canvas Rendering: We utilize the browser's native Canvas API (via GPU hardware acceleration where available) to perform pixel-level operations (resizing, rotations, and compression filters) locally on your device RAM.
  • jsPDF & pdf-lib Integration: PDF file compilation is executed dynamically inside your browser thread using standardized JavaScript libraries. No external REST APIs or remote document generation servers are used.
  • Zero-Server Architecture Benchmarks:
    • File upload latency: 0ms (Immediate local reading).
    • Typical conversion time: <150ms for single document, <500ms for batch photo-to-PDF operations.
    • Network dependency: Offline capable. Once loaded, the page works completely offline without active internet.
